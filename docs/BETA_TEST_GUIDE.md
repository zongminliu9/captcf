# CapTCF — Private Beta test guide

Thanks for helping test **CapTCF**, an independent study tool for the **TCF Canada** French exam.
It is a private beta, so a few things are rough and results are **practice estimates, not official
scores**.

> **Beta URL:** `__FILL_AFTER_DEPLOY__`  ← replaced with the real HTTPS link once the beta is live.

## Before you start

- **Browser:** latest **Chrome**, **Edge**, or **Safari**, on desktop or phone. The speaking module
  needs microphone access, which only works over the HTTPS link above.
- **Account:** none required — you can start practising as a guest. If you want your progress saved
  across devices, create an account with an email + a password. **Use a throwaway password you do
  not reuse anywhere else.**
- **Language:** the app interface is mostly French (the exam is in French); menus offer FR/EN/中文.

## Please don't enter

- No real banking, card, or payment information (payments are disabled — you'll see "Beta — paiements
  bientôt disponibles").
- No passwords you use on other sites, no government IDs, no sensitive personal data.
- Voice recordings are stored privately for your self-evaluation only and are deleted when you delete
  your account.

## Three flows to try first

1. **Guest quick practice.** From the home page, start practising in two clicks. Answer a listening
   question (play the audio), read the feedback and why the wrong options are wrong.
2. **Sign up + a mock section.** Register, open a full mock exam, and do the Listening + Reading
   sections. Refresh mid-section — your timer and answers should survive.
3. **Speaking + results.** Try a speaking task (allow the mic), record a short answer, and review the
   unified results page. Notice which scores are labelled auto-scored vs local self-evaluation.

## If something breaks

- Use **"Donner mon avis"** in the purple "Private Beta" strip at the top of any page — it sends your
  note straight to us.
- If a page shows an error, note the **URL**, what you clicked, and (if you can) take a screenshot.
- A blank screen or a raw error message is a bug worth reporting — that shouldn't happen.

## Known limitations (expected)

- Audio is **synthesised (text-to-speech)**, not human studio recordings — it's clear but robotic.
- Writing/speaking feedback is a **local rubric**, not AI grading; all scores are **unofficial**.
- On the free hosting tier the first visit after idle can take ~1 minute to wake, and voice
  recordings may not survive a redeploy.
- **Your beta data may be reset** during the beta period. Don't rely on it long-term.
